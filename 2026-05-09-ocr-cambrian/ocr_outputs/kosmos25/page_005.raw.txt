<bbox><x_85><y_615><x_431><y_633></bbox>We cast form ﬁeld detection as an object detection task

<bbox><x_85><y_825><x_431><y_842></bbox>putational efﬁciency of training and inference against per-
<bbox><x_85><y_842><x_431><y_860></bbox>formance; as resolution scales beyond this batch sizes de-
<bbox><x_85><y_860><x_277><y_877></bbox>crease and training times stretch.

<bbox><x_464><y_359><x_792><y_379></bbox>5.1. Resolution Matters In Form Field Detection
<bbox><x_464><y_386><x_810><y_404></bbox>Compared with objects in traditional object detection, many
<bbox><x_464><y_404><x_810><y_421></bbox>form ﬁelds are comparatively ﬁne in an average form. Cer-
<bbox><x_464><y_421><x_810><y_438></bbox>tain features that signify where a form element should go,
<bbox><x_464><y_438><x_810><y_456></bbox>such as underlines or colons, are also very ﬁne. A conse-
<bbox><x_464><y_456><x_810><y_473></bbox>quence of this is that form ﬁeld detection is more sensitive
<bbox><x_464><y_473><x_763><y_491></bbox>to input resolution than traditional object detection.
<bbox><x_481><y_492><x_810><y_509></bbox>We examine this by ﬁnetuning a series of 6 million pa-
<bbox><x_464><y_509><x_810><y_526></bbox>rameter FFDNet model on a dataset of 10k pages from
<bbox><x_464><y_526><x_572><y_544></bbox>C OMMON F ORMS .
<bbox><x_589><y_526><x_757><y_544></bbox>We compare 4 resolutions:
<bbox><x_770><y_526><x_810><y_544></bbox>640px,

<bbox><x_699><y_649><x_810><y_667></bbox>The differences in
