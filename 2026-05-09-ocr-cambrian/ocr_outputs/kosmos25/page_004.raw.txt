<bbox><x_85><y_694><x_277><y_715></bbox>3.2. Annotation Consistency
<bbox><x_85><y_722><x_431><y_740></bbox>Forms from the Web are not consistently annotated. De-
<bbox><x_85><y_740><x_431><y_757></bbox>spite extensive ﬁltering that reduces the candidate set of
<bbox><x_85><y_757><x_431><y_774></bbox>documents by more than 99%, there are still annotation in-
<bbox><x_85><y_774><x_431><y_792></bbox>consistencies in the prepared forms. These can negatively
<bbox><x_85><y_792><x_431><y_809></bbox>impact the real-world performance of any models trained
<bbox><x_85><y_809><x_431><y_827></bbox>on C OMMON F ORMS . We provide a representative, but not
<bbox><x_85><y_827><x_400><y_844></bbox>exhaustive, catalog of such inconsistencies in Table 1 .
<bbox><x_103><y_845><x_431><y_863></bbox>Some of these arise from unconventional or incorrect use
<bbox><x_85><y_863><x_431><y_880></bbox>of form elements. A common pattern is to see form ﬁelds
<bbox><x_85><y_880><x_431><y_897></bbox>used as headers and footers in a document. The automati-
<bbox><x_85><y_897><x_431><y_915></bbox>cally prepared forms can suffer from misleading heuristics,
<bbox><x_85><y_915><x_431><y_932></bbox>which often look for straight horizontal lines where form
<bbox><x_85><y_932><x_431><y_950></bbox>ﬁelds would likely be placed. This leads to the inclusion
<bbox><x_85><y_950><x_431><y_967></bbox>of spurious form ﬁelds. Text ﬁelds can be used in place of
<bbox><x_85><y_967><x_431><y_984></bbox>signature ﬁelds, or signature ﬁelds are left blank, intended
<bbox><x_85><y_984><x_372><y_1002></bbox>for a wet signature rather than a digital signature.
<bbox><x_103><y_1003><x_431><y_1020></bbox>However, some of these are related to the semantics of
<bbox><x_85><y_1020><x_431><y_1038></bbox>the form itself. “For Ofﬁcial Use Only” sections are some-
<bbox><x_464><y_577><x_667><y_594></bbox>times ﬁllable and sometimes not.
<bbox><x_680><y_577><x_810><y_594></bbox>Similarly, forms with
<bbox><x_464><y_594><x_810><y_611></bbox>“Circle All that Apply” sections are only occasionally in-
<bbox><x_464><y_611><x_757><y_629></bbox>teractive, even if the rest of the form is interactive.
<bbox><x_481><y_630><x_810><y_647></bbox>Qualitative results show that despite these inconsisten-
<bbox><x_464><y_647><x_810><y_665></bbox>cies, models trained on C OMMON F ORMS are eminently
<bbox><x_464><y_665><x_810><y_682></bbox>useful, even on complex forms. These systematic incon-
<bbox><x_464><y_682><x_810><y_700></bbox>sistencies provide an adverse training signal, and are a con-
<bbox><x_464><y_700><x_810><y_717></bbox>sequence of using scraped forms rather than manually an-
<bbox><x_464><y_717><x_810><y_734></bbox>notating a dataset. However, they are a trade-off made to
<bbox><x_464><y_734><x_810><y_752></bbox>scale the dataset to a practical size. We do not attempt to
<bbox><x_464><y_752><x_810><y_769></bbox>ﬁlter any of these annotation inconsistencies and leave that
<bbox><x_464><y_769><x_592><y_787></bbox>effort for future work.

<bbox><x_464><y_845><x_810><y_863></bbox>sual, such as an underline or empty box, there are many tex-
<bbox><x_464><y_863><x_810><y_880></bbox>tual cues as well. Cues such as colons before blank spaces,
<bbox><x_464><y_880><x_810><y_897></bbox>circle or check all that apply sequences, and column headers
<bbox><x_464><y_897><x_810><y_915></bbox>all use text to indicate the presence of a form ﬁeld. There

<bbox><x_464><y_932><x_810><y_950></bbox>and left-to-right language. As such, models trained to de-
<bbox><x_464><y_950><x_810><y_967></bbox>tect form ﬁelds beneﬁt from a large number of examples for
<bbox><x_464><y_967><x_551><y_984></bbox>each language.
